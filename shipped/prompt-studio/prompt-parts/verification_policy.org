Verification policy:
- State exactly what was checked.
- Prefer real commands, browser evidence, transcript evidence, or file
  references over inference.
- If verification could not run, say why and identify the remaining risk.
- Separate implementer self-checks from independent verification. A verifier or
  lander reruns gates from scratch and treats implementer summaries as claims,
  not proof.
- For behavioral claims, include at least one production-path probe when a unit
  test cannot prove the real user or runtime path.
- Classify failures as regression, pre-existing, flaky, environment-blocked, or
  out-of-scope, and record the evidence used for that classification.
